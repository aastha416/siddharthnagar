HISTORY OF SIDDHAARTHNAGAR

         SIDDHAARTHNAGAR district lies between    270 N to 270 28 N and 820 45 E to 830 10 E

         near the north eastern border of  UTTAR PRADESH. 

         History of the district is assoctiated with the life oF GAUTAM BUDDHA.

          This district has been named after the name of GAUTAM BUDDHA's childhood name

           Prince "siddhartha". 

         In the past area was covered with forests of Himalayan foothills was part of SHAKYA DYNASTY.

         In the 6thcentury BC the Shakyons built their capital in KAPILVASTUand established a powerful
         republic here.
         
         PIPERHAVA STUPAwas excavated by W.C.

         Several important events related to the life of GAUTAM BUDDHA occur in this area.
        
         In KAPILVASTU  RAJ PRASAD  ruins of BUDDHIST BIHARS BUILTS in the era of BUDDHA and
         the mortal remains of SHAKYA MUNI.

         NO.-5-4 (4)/76-135-00-5(b) dated DECEMBER 1988.
